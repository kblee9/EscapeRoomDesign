The Hidden Door


After you reach the end of the string, you see that the wall in front of you features an intricate carving with 8 stones jutting out of the wall beneath it. It appears to be a puzzle of some sort. You reach out to press in one of the stones, but it does not seem to stay put. Hmm... perhaps only certain stones are meant to be pressed. You go on to inspect the carving in the wall and recognize it to be the image of a 7 circuit maze from ancient cretan coins. You make an educated guess that the key to the puzzle has something to do with the labyrinth, so you ask one of your partners about the myth.
They tell you this:

The story of the Labyrinth begins on the island of Crete, where King Minos had the famous inventor Daedalus build the 7 dimensional maze to trap the Minotaur, his illegitimate son who is half bull and hald human. While the interval varies between versions, the story says that every 7 years, Minos sends a group of 7 men and 7 women into the Labyrinth to be fed to the Minotaur.
